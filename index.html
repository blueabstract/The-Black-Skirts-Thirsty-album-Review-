Album Description

The album “Thirsty” by The Black Skirts revolves around themes of love, longing, and introspection.
It delves into the complexities of human emotions, capturing both the euphoria and the melancholy that come with love and relationships.
The songs often reflect a sense of yearning and the bittersweet nature of memories and experiences.


About Artist

The Black Skirts is the stage name of Jo Hyu-il (also known as Bryan Cho), an indie rock musician from South Korea. Here are some key details about him: Early Life: Jo Hyu-il was born on December 5, 1982, in Seoul, South Korea. He moved to the United States at the age of 12 and grew up in New Jersey12. His musical tastes were influenced by American music media, such as MTV and Rolling Stone1.

Musical Beginnings: Before becoming The Black Skirts, Jo was part of a punk rock band called Castel Prayon in 2004 while living in New York City1. After the band dissolved, he returned to South Korea and began his solo career.
Debut and Success: He released his debut album, “201”, in 2010, which won the Best Modern Rock Album at the Korean Music Awards1. This album was recorded in his basement and marked his entry into the Korean indie rock scene.
Subsequent Albums: Jo has released several albums since his debut, including “Don’t You Worry Baby (I’m Only Swimming)” (2011), “Team Baby” (2017), “Thirsty” (2019), and “Teen Troubles” (2022)12. Each album showcases his unique blend of indie rock, folk, and pop influences.

Musical Style: His music is known for its emotional depth, introspective lyrics, and a mix of nostalgic and contemporary sounds. He often explores themes of love, longing, and personal reflection.

Recognition: The Black Skirts has received critical acclaim and several awards, highlighting his impact on the indie music scene in South Korea1.

Personal Touch: Jo’s music often reflects his personal experiences and emotions, making it relatable and heartfelt for his listeners. He continues to be an influential figure in the indie music community.
